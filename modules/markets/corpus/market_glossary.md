# Market Reference Glossary

EBITDA stands for earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization. It is a measure of a company's operating profitability before the effect of financing and accounting decisions, and is often used to compare companies with different capital structures.

EPS, or earnings per share, is a company's net income divided by its number of outstanding shares. Diluted EPS additionally accounts for options, warrants, and convertible securities that could increase the share count.

The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio is the share price divided by earnings per share. It expresses how much investors are willing to pay for each dollar of earnings.

Gross margin is revenue minus cost of goods sold, divided by revenue. It measures how much of each sales dollar remains after the direct costs of producing the goods or services.
