# GDPR - plain-language summary

Data subjects have several rights, including the right of access to their personal data, the right to rectification of inaccurate data, the right to erasure (the "right to be forgotten"), and the right to data portability.

Processing of personal data must rest on a lawful basis, such as the data subject's consent, the performance of a contract, a legal obligation, or a legitimate interest that is not overridden by the data subject's rights.

In the event of a personal data breach, the data controller must notify the relevant supervisory authority without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.
